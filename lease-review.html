Why It Matters

Portuguese Leases Can Be Tricky for Newcomers

Rental contracts in Portugal often contain clauses that are unfamiliar — or even unfair — to international tenants. Language barriers make it worse. Without local knowledge, you might agree to terms that don't protect you, or miss red flags that could cost you thousands of euros.

Our review gives you clarity and confidence before you sign anything.

What's Included

A Complete Contract Health Check

We examine every aspect of your lease that could affect your tenancy experience or financial exposure.

Financial Terms

Rent amount, payment schedule, deposit requirements, rent increase mechanisms, and any additional fees or charges buried in the fine print.

Duration & Renewal

Contract length, automatic renewal conditions, notice periods, and what happens if you need to leave early or extend your stay.

Maintenance & Repairs

Who's responsible for what, how repairs should be requested, timelines for landlord response, and any unfair maintenance obligations.

Your Rights & Protections

Privacy rights, entry notification requirements, what the landlord can and cannot do, and how well the contract protects you as a tenant.

Red Flags & Risks

Unusual clauses, potentially illegal terms, one-sided provisions, and anything that could put you at a disadvantage or legal risk.

Negotiation Points

Specific recommendations on clauses you should try to change, with suggested alternative language you can propose to your landlord.
